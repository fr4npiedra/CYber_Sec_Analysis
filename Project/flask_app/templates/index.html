Business Problem

The amount of traffic moving over the network increases everyday, making most companies and institutions vulnerable to malicious packages coming their way. The purpose of our project was to create a predictive model that could identify these packages and classify them as either normal or suspicious.


Data

The dataset used is from the Intrusion Detection Evaluation Program which was prepared and managed by MIT Lincoln Labs. Lincoln Labs set up an environment to acquire nine weeks of raw TCP dump data for a local-area network (LAN) simulating a typical U.S. Air Force LAN.
We used 15 features:
srv_count; same_srv_rate; diff_srv_rate; srv_diff_host_rate; dst_host_count; dst_host_srv_count; dst_host_same_srv_rate; dst_host_diff_srv_rate; dst_host_same_src_port_rate; dst_host_srv_diff_host_rate; dst_host_serror_rate; dst_host_srv_serror_rate; protocol_type; service; flag.
And 1 independent variable: “class”.
